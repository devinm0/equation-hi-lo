How to Play Equation Hi Lo

Equation Hi Lo is a poker-style card game played with math instead of poker hands. You are dealt number cards (0–10) and operator cards (+, −, ×, ÷, √), and you arrange them into an equation. Your goal is a result as close as possible to either 1 (Low) or 20 (High). A hand plays out in the order below.

1. The deal & first bet

To start, you receive two face-up number cards and one hidden card that only you can see (it's revealed at showdown), along with operator cards to combine them. A first round of betting then goes around the table.

Everyone always gets the +, − and ÷ operators. On top of those, you may also be dealt a × (multiply) or a √ (square root) card.

2. The multiply & root cards

If you're dealt a multiply (×) card, you must discard one of three cards: the ×, the +, or the −. You may never discard the ÷. The game highlights your three choices, just like below — tap one to discard it.

3. Second card & equation forming

After the first bet, you're dealt one more face-up card. Then the equation-forming phase begins under a countdown timer. Drag your cards left and right to order them into the equation you want, then lock in before the timer runs out. A valid equation gives a real number:

Division by zero is rejected — the cards flash red until you fix it. If the timer runs out before you form a valid equation, you're automatically folded.

4. Second bet

With equations locked, a second round of betting takes place. You can call, raise, go all-in, or fold.

No player may bet more than it would take for the player with the fewest chips to go all in — bets are capped at the shortest stack. There are no side pots like in regular poker, because the pot is already potentially split between the High and Low winners.

5. Choosing Low, High, or Swing

Finally, each remaining player bets on a side. Low means your result is closest to 1; High means closest to 20. Importantly, Low targets 1, not 0 — a result of 1.25 (0.25 away from 1) beats a result of 0.25 (0.75 away from 1).

A Swing bet claims both pots. Players who go Swing then form a second equation from their cards — one aimed at 1 and one aimed at 20.

6. Showdown & winning

At showdown the Low pot goes to whoever is closest to 1, and the High pot goes to whoever is closest to 20.

A Swing bet must win both sides. If a Swing player wins Low but loses High (or vice-versa), they win nothing — and the side they did win passes to the next-closest player who bet that side, if any.

Ties. If players are an equal distance from the target:

  • Low: the win goes to the player holding the lowest single number card; if that's also tied, the lower suit wins.
  • High: the mirror image — the win goes to the highest single number card; if tied, the higher suit wins.
